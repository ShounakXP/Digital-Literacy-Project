# Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords or bank details.

A common example is when a student receives an email that appears to be from a bank or university. The email may contain a link asking the user to verify their account details. Once the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks legitimate. When they enter their credentials, the attacker captures the information.

Students are often targeted because they frequently use email and online platforms. Many are unaware of such scams and may act quickly without verifying the source.

The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking links and verifying sources before sharing sensitive information.
